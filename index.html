I am a computational neuroscientist based in Prague, Czechia at the Faculty of Mathematics and Physics of Charles University.

The goal of my research is to identify computations implemented in the neural system that underly our sensory perception. To that end, my group uses various computational techniques including but not limited to machine learning and large-scale biophysical neural modeling. We build models of visual system at different levels of abstraction. Through confrontation of these models with biological data we further our understanding of visual processing and drive future experiments towards most informative questions.

We clollaborate closely with the experimental group of Yves Frégnac, the theoretical group of Alain Destexhe and the neuroinformatics group of Andrew Davison at NeuroPSI, CNRS, France as well as experimental group of Frédéric Chavane at the Timone Neuroscience Institute, Marseille.
